History of the Intergalaxians

  • Late 70s launch of the Intergalaxian toy line
  • Early 80s first comic book series
  • Late Early 80s a short-lived Saturday morning cartoon
  • Late 90s edgy second comic book series
  • Mid 00s pastel cartoon reboot that offends the delicate sensibilities of regressive "fans"
  • Early 20s the first Intergalaxian tabletop role-playing game

Previously On

  • An imaginary toy line about interstellar warriors that can swap their various parts
  • A desire to scratch that nostalgia itch in both setting and rules aesthetic
  • Build your Intergalaxians via a card drafting mechanism
  • Perhaps rolling dice to build up totals until you exceed a target number
  • Ready to abandon any of this

Original Toy Line

A giant assortment of random arms, torsos, heads, and lower bodies that can snap together.

  • No moving parts except where each interchangeable attaches.
  • No paint. Each interchangeable is a solid plastic color.
    • Sold in lots of different colors to reuse molds, including glow-in-the-dark and scented interchangeables later on.
  • All interchangeables attach to the torso.

Intergalaxian Body Composition

Head

Knowledge, history, experience.

Torso

Value, armor

Right Arm

Skills, prowess

Left Arm

Cunning, ambush

Lower Body

Movement, should always lift with your legs, carry burdens

Death and Dying

Intergalaxians cannot die. But they can be blown apart, and their interchangeable parts can be scattered to the five corners of the universe.
